**Body:**

There's a seven-level model of AI maturity that explains why most professionals stay stuck. Once you see the map, two things happen: you realize how far you've come, and you realize how far there is to go.

---

## Screen 2 — Information

**Heading:** The Seven Levels of AI Maturity

**Body:**

**Level 1: Fancy search engine** — Ask one-off questions. Get answers. Close the tab. Most professionals never leave this level.

**Level 2: Prompting on purpose** — You notice how you ask changes what you get. You structure prompts. You save the ones that work.

**Level 3: AI woven into your work** — You stopped starting fresh every conversation. You have custom workspaces. AI feels intuitive.

**Level 4: Multi-tool ecosystem** — Route between tools. Build small interactive tools with Artifacts. Get your first taste of AI agents.

**Level 5: Things that run without you** — Automated workflows triggered by events. Real systems running while you sleep.

**Level 6: You see systems everywhere** — Claude Code as a building tool. The line between "user" and "builder" stops mattering.

**Level 7: AI workforce** — Aspirational. Someday a single founder builds with an AI workforce running everything.

---

## Screen 3 — Information

**Heading:** You finished M5 at Level 3. Most professionals you work with are still at Level 1 or 2.

**Body:**

Here's the part nobody tells you: **you now have all the foundations. From here on, 99% of further progress is practice. Not new techniques. Not better tools. Not the next course. Just practice** — running what you already know on real work, week after week, until the fluency consolidates.

This mission is about that consolidation. The four lessons after this one — reusable prompts, workflows, organization, weekly practice — are the moves that turn five missions of fluency into something you'll still be running in eighteen months.

## Screen 4 — Knowledge (MCQ)

**Heading:** Where do they sit?

**Subheading:** A peer says: "I use AI all the time. I open ChatGPT a few times a week for emails or research. It's saved me time." They have no custom workspaces or saved prompts.

**Options** (4):

- **A. Level 1 — using AI as a fancy search engine** _(correct)_
- B. Level 2 — they use AI regularly
- C. Between 2 and 3 — they're past one-off use

**Explanation:**

Level transitions aren't about frequency — they're about how you use. One-off questions with no structure, no workspaces. That's level 1 regardless of how often they open it.
